# Generation-5 Betavoltaic Substrate Shootout

## Final Conclusions

A. Best theoretical substrate: **Diamond** with honeycomb_mesh and C14.
B. Best manufacturable substrate: **Diamond** with straight_fingers.
C. Best low-cost substrate: **Silicon** with straight_fingers.
D. Best commercialization candidate: **Silicon** with baseline; best graphene-assisted commercialization candidate: **Silicon** with straight_fingers.
E. Best laboratory prototype candidate: **Diamond** with laser_graphitic_channels.
F. Best substrate for embedded graphene fingers: **Diamond**.
G. Best substrate for interdigitated graphene combs: **Diamond**.
H. Diamond remains optimal after manufacturing constraints: **YES**.
I. 4H-SiC, GaN, or another substrate overtakes diamond in absolute manufacturable power: **NO**; silicon overtakes diamond in cost-adjusted performance.

## Special Investigations

- Substrates surpassing diamond after manufacturing constraints: none.
- Substrates at >=80% of diamond manufacturable performance and <=25% of diamond cost: none.
- Laser-written graphitic channels are physically applicable only for diamond in this screening model.

## Interpretation

- Diamond keeps the strongest physics position because radiation tolerance, thermal conductivity, and breakdown field protect CCE and lifetime.
- Silicon is the cost disruptor: cheap and mature, but low radiation tolerance and low breakdown voltage keep it from matching diamond physics.
- 4H-SiC is the most credible non-diamond commercialization challenger because it combines maturity, wide bandgap behavior, radiation hardness, and lower cost.
- GaN is competitive for prototype work but loses ground from defect sensitivity and graphene integration difficulty.
- Laser-written graphitic channels are treated as diamond-specific; they do not transfer directly to SiC, GaN, AlN, beta-Ga2O3, silicon, or c-BN.
