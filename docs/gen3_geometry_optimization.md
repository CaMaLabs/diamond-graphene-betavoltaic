# Generation 3 — Embedded Collector Geometry Optimization

## Question

Which internal graphene or graphitic collector geometry improves charge collection without adding enough interface and trap area to erase the gain?

## Geometries

- Straight parallel fingers
- Interdigitated combs
- Honeycomb mesh
- Radial spokes
- Fractal/tree networks
- Multilayer mesh
- Random percolating nanoribbon network

## Outcome

Interdigitated combs produced the highest raw simulated power. Straight fingers were the minimum-change geometry that beat baseline while preserving manufacturability.

## Representative Practical Geometry

```text
straight fingers
pitch: about 12 micrometers
width: about 15 nanometers
single layer
depth fraction: about 0.35
```

## Design Lesson

Simple internal collectors may be more valuable than complex dense meshes because they reduce carrier travel distance while limiting additional trap/interface area.
