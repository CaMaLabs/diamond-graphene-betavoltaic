# Generation 4 — Manufacturability Overlay

## Question

Which internal collector geometry remains attractive after manufacturing constraints are included?

## Constraints Considered

- Minimum feature size
- Alignment tolerance
- Fabrication yield
- Process maturity
- Added defect density
- Interface roughness
- Cost
- Scalability

## Outcome

The theoretical winner remained a dense comb-style collector. The best practical architecture was straight internal graphene or graphitic fingers.

A simplified graphitic-channel demonstrator may be useful as an early validation path, but the long-term target remains a controlled internal finger collector geometry.

## Design Lesson

A less aggressive collector layout can win after yield and process penalties.
