# Prototype Fabrication Plan

## Preferred First Prototype

A non-radioactive surrogate demonstrator:

```text
safe carrier-generation surrogate
↓
commercial CVD diamond sample or detector-grade diamond
↓
surface or shallow graphitic collector fingers
↓
metal contacts
↓
low-noise measurement circuit
```

## Route A — Safe Surrogate Demonstrator

Purpose: test whether graphitic or graphene-like collector geometry improves charge collection.

- Use non-radioactive excitation.
- Compare planar contacts against fingered collectors.
- Measure I-V response, leakage, contact resistance, and signal gain.

## Route B — Graphitic Channel Demonstrator

Purpose: test whether graphitic channels can behave as collector fingers without destroying diamond quality.

- Use commercial diamond.
- Use professional microfabrication access.
- Characterize resistance and leakage before any source-based testing.

## Route C — Professional Validation

Purpose: validate radiovoltaic behavior under licensed, regulated conditions.

- Only in licensed facilities.
- Use approved measurement setups.
- Validate source utilization and long-term stability.

## Recommended First Experiment

Compare planar diamond electrodes versus fingered graphitic or graphene-like collectors under a safe surrogate excitation source.

## Go/No-Go Criteria

Proceed only if the collector geometry shows:

- higher collected signal at equal excitation,
- acceptable leakage current,
- stable contact resistance,
- no catastrophic defect-induced degradation.
