# Measurement Plan

## Purpose

Test whether internal or surface fingered graphitic collectors improve charge collection compared with planar contacts under safe surrogate excitation.

## Measurements

- Open-circuit voltage
- Short-circuit current
- I-V curve
- Dark leakage current
- Contact resistance
- Temperature dependence
- Time stability
- Carrier-collection proxy under surrogate excitation

## Comparison Matrix

| Test Device | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Planar diamond electrodes | Reference baseline |
| Straight finger collectors | Primary candidate |
| Graphitic-channel collectors | Practical demonstrator |
| Dense comb collectors | High-risk/high-gain comparison |

## Fastest Falsification Test

If fingered collectors do not improve collected signal under controlled surrogate excitation compared with planar contacts, the internal-collector hypothesis is weakened.

## Result That Justifies Moving Forward

A repeatable collection improvement with acceptable leakage and contact resistance justifies more detailed fabrication planning and professional validation.
